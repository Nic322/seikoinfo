About Seiko

Seiko is a industry titan in the watchmaking space, based in Japan. It was founded in 1881 by Kintaro Hattori.
They played an extremely importantant role of developing quartz technology. Their watches are well known to be extremely reliable and precise.
They create Siving Watches, Spring Drive watches, and solar powered watches.

How Automatic Watches Work

Winding The Main Spring

Winding the crown stores the energy in the mainspring. The winding tightens the mainspring and stores potential energy in the spring.



Transference Through The Movement

As the spring unwinds it transfers the energy gradually through the movement.



The Escapement

The energy is then regulated in the escapement so it is not released all at once.



The rotating wheel of the escapement is called the balance wheel. It gradually releases the energy to the hands of the watch.

Seiko's History

1881

Kintaro Hattori opens a watch and jewelry shop in Tokyo.

1892

The first factory named Seikosha is established, initially producing wall clocks.

1895

Seiko produces its first pocket watch, named the "Timekeeper".

1913

Seiko produces its first wristwatch.

1924

The first Seiko branded wristwatch is introduced

1960

The first Grand Seiko is announced, which was well known for its high accuracy.

1969

The world's first quartz watch is introduced.

2005

Seiko releases the world's first E-ink watch.

2012

Seiko releases the Astorn GPS Solar, the world's first solar watch.
